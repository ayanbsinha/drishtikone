COVID-19 : Boost Your Immunity

So far we don't have cure or vaccinations for Corona virus disease or COVID-19. It has been observed people with poor immunity are getting hit badly by this specific strain of virus. Although we are doing well by taking measures like social distance, proper hygiene by washing hands with soap or with alcohol based sanitizer and using various disinfectants.As we understand that diet plays very important role to keep our immune system at its best. During the period of lock down it might be little difficult to find all fruits, vegetables and grains that is required to maintain a strong immune system through out this period.I will be sharing few supplement that can help you to boost your immunity.

  • Vitamins & Minerals

    Vitamins and minerals are essential substances that our bodies need to develop and function normally. The known vitamins include A, C, D, E, and K, and the B vitamins: thiamin (B1), riboflavin (B2), niacin (B3), pantothenic acid (B5), pyridoxal (B6), cobalamin (B12), biotin, and folate/folic acid. A number of minerals are essential for health: calcium, phosphorus, potassium, sodium, chloride, magnesium, iron, zinc, iodine, sulfur, cobalt, copper, fluoride, manganese, and selenium.A Balance diet is always recommend for a healthy life style.
    Today I am writing about the vitamins, minerals and activities that will help to boost you immunity.
  • Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid) Vitamin C is one of the safest and most effective nutrients. Benefits of vitamin C may include protection against immune system deficiencies, cardiovascular disease, prenatal health problems, eye disease, and even skin wrinkling. Vitamin C is found naturally in vegetables and fruits, especially oranges and other citrus fruits.
  • Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine) Pyridoxine is required for the proper function of sugars, fats, and proteins in the body. It is also required for the proper growth and development of the brain, nerves, skin, and many other parts of the body. It has many other medical benefits. Some of the best sources of B6 include beans, poultry, fish, fortified cereals, dark leafy greens, papayas, oranges and cantaloupe, according to the Harvard School of Public Health (HSPH). Bananas are another good source.
  • Resveratrol Resveratrol is a great antioxidant and improves body's immune response to infection. However, if you're on any anticoagulant or blood thinners, you should skip this one as it slows down blood clotting. People having hormone-sensitive condition should not take resveratrol because it has phytoestrogens. Those having regular medicines should check with their doctor for any contraindications. It's found in foods such as peanuts, pistachios, grapes,blueberries, cranberries, and even cocoa and dark chocolate.
  • Vitamin E Vitamin E is a powerful antioxidant that helps the body fight off infection. Foods rich in Vitamin E includes nuts, seeds & spinach
  • Zinc Zinc is an anti-inflammatory and support to build stronger immune system.
  • Activities for Immunity Apart of the mentioned supplements there are other activities which should be taken care of which can help you to boost you immunity along with physical and mental wellness during this period of pandemic.
  • Sleep During sleep, your immune system releases proteins called cytokines, some of which help promote sleep. Certain cytokines need to increase when you have an infection or inflammation, or when you're under stress. Sleep deprivation may decrease production of these protective cytokines. In addition, infection-fighting antibodies and cells are reduced during periods when you don't get enough sleep. The optimal amount of sleep for most adults is seven to eight hours of good sleep each night. Teenagers need nine to 10 hours of sleep. School-aged children may need 10 or more hours of sleep. But more sleep isn't always better.
